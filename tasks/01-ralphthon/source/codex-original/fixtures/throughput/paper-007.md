# Mock Paper 007: Privacy-Preserving Count Release

Synthetic fixture; not a real paper or experimental result.

## Abstract (page 1)

The study applies Laplace noise to release aggregate counts and evaluates the utility-privacy tradeoff. It claims practical privacy protection for small organizations.

## Method (page 2)

Noise scale is calibrated to a stated sensitivity of one and epsilon values from 0.1 to 2.0. The neighboring-dataset relation is defined for adding or removing one record.

## Results (page 3)

Table 1 gives median absolute error for synthetic counts, including 9.8 at epsilon 0.1 and 0.6 at epsilon 2.0. No composition analysis is provided for repeated weekly releases.

## Limitations (page 4)

The mechanism protects individual records but not attributes inferable from external information. Operational key management is not addressed.
